Introduction

Manta rays are large, graceful marine animals belonging to the genus Mobula. Known for their impressive wingspans—some reaching up to 7 meters (23 feet)—they are among the largest rays in the ocean. Despite their size, manta rays are harmless filter feeders, feeding mainly on plankton and small fish by swimming with their mouths open.

  • There are two main species:
    • Reef manta ray (Mobula alfredi) usually found in coastal waters near coral reefs.
    • Giant oceanic manta ray (Mobula birostris) often found in open oceans and capable of migrating long distances.
  • Key Features:
    • Distinctive "wings" (pectoral fins) used to gracefully glide through the water. Cephalic lobes, which help funnel plankton into their mouths. High intelligence, with one of the largest brain-to-body ratios of any fish.
